# Key Quantities

## Position
Position is the description of an object's location with respect to a defined reference point and coordinate system at a given time.

In a 2D Cartesian coordinate system,

$$
  \boxed{\mathbf r = \mathbf r (x,y) = x \hat{\mathbf i} + y \hat{\mathbf j}}
$$

In a 3D Cartesian coordinate system,

$$
  \boxed{\mathbf r = \mathbf r (x,y,z) = x \hat{\mathbf i} + y \hat{\mathbf j} + z \hat{\mathbf k}}
$$

In a polar coordinate system,

$$
  \boxed{\mathbf r = \mathbf r (r, \theta) = r \hat{\mathbf r}}
$$

In a cylindrical coordinate system,

$$
  \mathbf r = \mathbf r (r, \theta, z)
$$

In a spherical coordinate system,

$$
  \mathbf r = \mathbf r (r, \theta, \varphi)
$$

## Displacement
Displacement is the shift in location when an object in motion changes from one position to another.

$$
  \boxed{\Delta \mathbf r = \mathbf r - \mathbf r_0}
$$

The infinitesimal displacement is defined as

$$
  \boxed{\mathrm d \mathbf r = \lim_{\mathbf r \to \mathbf r_0} \Delta \mathbf r}
$$

The magnitude of the infinitesimal displacement is the infinitesimal arc length, which is defined as

$$
  \boxed{\mathrm d s = |\mathrm d \mathbf r|}
$$

The amount of path traveled by a particle in space is called the distance and it is represented by the total arc length,

$$
  \mathbf s = \int \mathrm d s
$$
